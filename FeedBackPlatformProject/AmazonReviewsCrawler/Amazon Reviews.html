Cooks Illustrated just did a review of knife sharpeners and this model came out tops. 5.0 out of 5 starsGreat sharpener but see first sentence of review for caveat
ByRainmanPon September 6, 2018
As great as this sharpener is, and as much as I love it, I have to point out for anyone interested that this sharpener will re-grind a 20 degree bevel to 15, but it will not sharpen knives you want to keep at 20 degrees. For that you want to get the Chef's Choice 1520. Since I NEVER try to cut anything hard, like trying to cleaver a chicken bone, this really does not matter to me. I plan to leave a couple of knives at 20 degrees for disjointing chickens, though even those should involve cutting only tendons or perhaps a bit of cartilage. Just be aware of this limitation if it matters to you.

This sharpener does an AMAZING job! Some knives may have a thicker primary bevel and require a lot more work in the first sharpening stage. If you think your knife is sharp but you cannot slice a tomato almost paper thin, you can do better. I have an all stainless steel Chinese chopping knife (the kind that looks like a cleaver) that has been my favorite knife for more than 30 years. I thought I had gotten it pretty sharp, but when I got another knife even sharper I decided to give it another go. WOW! I would not say it is "razor" sharp, but it is as close to it as a kitchen knife needs to be. My test is how well a knife slices tomatoes because if it will easily slice a tomato, it will handle everything I need. I can use the same little grape tomato all day because the knives get sharp enough to slice tiny slices. I got carried away slicing some leftover roast pork tenderloin the other day to season for fajitas before my wife reminded me that the slices could be much thicker than 1/16"! I just kept going thinner and thinner to see how the knife would do. You would not think it would make that much difference on something so simple, but just cutting up the peppers and onions for the fajitas was effortless.

Since my Boy Scout days 50 years ago, I have always been a stickler for sharp knives. I always thought my knives were pretty sharp, and they were compared to those in most kitchens, but I am amazed at how much sharper they are now.

I sharpen with a light touch, putting only enough pressure to maintain good contact with the wheels. On my big Chinese chopper, this means almost no pressure at all. On smaller knives it means using slight pressure. You should never feel like you are really pressing down on the blade. It is an adage in woodworking to "let the tool do the work." Putting excess pressure reduces the efficiency of the tool and can cause accidents.

5.0 out of 5 starsBe careful - this REALLY sharpens your knives!
ByDocdaddyon September 5, 2018
Verified Purchase
We don't have super high quality knives, and while we would like to purchase a $500 set of knives, it's not in our current plans. So I purchased this, after reading reviews in cooking magazines and Wirecutter, and pulled out all of our knives to sharpen.

This comes with a warning: Your knives will be sharper than before so be careful. Yeah, yeah, great marketing. I get it, it takes your normal 20 degree edges for less expensive knives and sharpens them to 15 degrees. Fine, I just want to be able to cut a tomato instead of tearing it.

Well, pay attention to the warning. I took a knife after sharpening it with this, held a tomato in my hand (I know, stupid sign on my head) and cut it. The knife went through it so smoothly and quickly and easily I didn't even notice it kept going into my ring finger. Even then, it was sharp enough I didn't even really feel the cut in my finger at first, I just notice a line and blood!

This sharpener, if you follow the instructions, will truly make your knives SHARP. Sharp enough that I've cut my hand three times because I'm not used to having to be as careful as you have to be with REALLY sharp knives. We converted a set of relatively inexpensive Cuisinart knives into a very effective knife set. No, they aren't a $600 set of knives now but I wouldn't want them any sharper!

Exceeded my expectations.
